Pan de muerto (Day of the dead buns)

Ingredients

  • 60 ml warm water
  • 1 pack of dry yeast
  • 650 gr all porpouse flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup of condensed milk
  • 1 ts salt
  • 1 ts vanilla escence
  • 12 gr orange zest
  • 150 gr sugar
  • 200 gr butter

Directions

  1. Mix yeast and a pinch of sugar into lukewarm water.

  2. Add flour, eggs, condensed milk, salt, vanilla, orange zest and yeast mixture, in a mixer with a hook attached. Mix in low speed for 2 minutes, then increase speed and mix for at least 5 minutes.

  3. After 5 minutes add butter in small batches and continue mixing until dough is smooth and not sticky.

  4. Leave the dough to proof for 3 hours, or until it doubles its size.

  5. Divide the dough in to 5 equal pieces and knead the pieces to form balls.

  6. Leave four of the balls to proof. With the fifth ball make small balls and long sticks to create “bones” that will decorate the other 4 buns. Let the buns rest another 20 min.

  7. Brush the decorated buns with egg wash and bake in a preheated oven at 180° C for 18 to 20 minutes or until golden brown.

  8. Let buns cool down and then brush them with warm butter and sprinkle with sugar.

More about "Pan de muerto"

"The origin of the Pan de Muerto dates back to the time of the Conquest, which was inspired by pre-Hispanic rituals when human sacrifices were practiced and which today is one of the major representatives of the Day of the Dead The creation of this bread was due to the fact that the Spanish found this practice very violent, so they suggested preparing a wheat bread covered with red sugar, which simulated the hearts of the maidens without reaching the extremes of taking their lives away. It is known that in Mesoamerica a ground amaranth bread was prepared that, after mixing with the blood of the sacrifices, was offered to the gods, these being the first signs of the Bread of the Dead, which has been modified until it becomes what we know today, and has also become one of the most important components of the offerings.
What does it represent?
The circle in the center symbolizes the skull of the deceased and the raised strips that are covered around it are the imitation of the bones that make up the human body; in some cases orange blossom essence is added, which evokes the memory of the deceased." Perhaps too creepy but yet delicious!
